Driving Thrills

About

Open world game that has action game and adventure. This 3D game having Indian cars combines the best of free roam game dynamics with immersive crime simulator action. this gangster game offers a thrilling urban adventure filled stunts, vehicles, challenges, game missions and cheats.

Traffic System

A modular system that populates and manages AI vehicles along waypoint paths, adapting density and performance to user graphics settings.

Core Responsibilities:

  • Graphics-Driven Density
    • It reads Settings.GraphicsIndex to Configurable MaxSpawnCount per quality level
  • Object Pooling
    • Pre-instantiates VehicleAIs and stored them in vehiclePool and Reuses pooled vehicles when escaping
  • Waypoint-Based Spawning
    • Finds all WaypointPath instances within MaxSpawnRadius of the player
    • Samples their waypoints at intervals of spaceBetweenVehicles to evenly distribute spawns
    • Computes spawn rotation from current → next waypoint for natural orientation
  • Player-Vehicle Interaction
    • Removes a vehicle AI from the pool when the player enters via the OnPlayerEnterAiCar event
    • On explosion of the vehicle AI, updates the pool to replenish traffic
  • Performance Culling
    • To make sure traffic dosen't spawn in front of the player they only spawn far away or behind the player likewise vehicle AIs that wander out of range or fall behind the player after a set distance are despawned

Police System

In this system, players build up Wanted Stars (0 to 5) based on the crimes they commit. The more chaos you cause, the more stars you attract. Once your crime rate crosses a threshold, you earn a star—and with it comes trouble: sirens, police cars, and police NPCs chasing you down.
Only way out is to escape the police.

Tech Notes:

  • Event-Driven Architecture: It’s all powered by WantedStarSystem that tracks your wanted level and fires events when crime is committed such as OnWantedStarAdded(int wantedLevel) and OnResetWantedStar(). The WantedLevelManager ties it all together—handling crime thresholds, triggering police spawners, and cleaning up when you escape or get busted.
  • Object Pooling: performant instantiation using delays and recycling of police entities when escaping.
  • Adaptive Spawning:
    • Singleton Persistence: Inherits from MonoBehaviourSingletonPersistentNoLoad for scene changes
    • Coroutines for Smooth Performance: Delays in both spawning and death cleanup
    • NavMesh SamplePosition: Spawn Police AIs at a random position in the navMesh area such as road to avoid overlaps on obstacle and gives clear paths to player.
    • Player-View Exclusion: Simple dot-product trick keeps Ais spawning behind the player or at a far distance.
